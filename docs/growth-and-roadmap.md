# Artemis Moon Growth and Roadmap Plan

Last updated: 28 June 2026

This document is a working reference for developing Artemis Moon as a long-term project rather than a short presale campaign. It combines the next practical website and traffic steps with a public-facing roadmap narrative that can be refined for the website, whitepaper, socials and investor communications.

Artemis Moon is an independent Ethereum token project and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by NASA. Roadmap items are objectives, not guarantees.

## Public Roadmap Narrative

The roadmap should show that Artemis Moon is built for longevity: first as a presale and launch project, then as a community token, then as a utility layer for space-themed auctions, merchandise, memorabilia and a future marketplace.

### Phase 1: Presale and Verified Foundations

Objective: create a transparent onchain foundation before the main Artemis attention cycle.

Key goals:

- Deploy the ARMN ERC-20 token on Ethereum.
- Deploy and verify the presale contract.
- Deploy and fund the team lock.
- Publish the whitepaper and Control Centre.
- Complete Etherscan token profile.
- Build email capture and community channels.
- Grow SEO traffic through mission content.

Positioning:

Artemis Moon begins with transparency: fixed supply, verified contracts, public tokenomics and a presale that can be inspected onchain.

### Phase 2: Public Launch and Uniswap Liquidity

Target timing: around H2 2027, before the expected Artemis III mission window, subject to market conditions and project readiness.

Key goals:

- Complete presale phase.
- Activate claims when appropriate.
- Launch ARMN trading on Uniswap.
- Create an initial liquidity pool.
- Publish liquidity information clearly.
- Continue mission-led content and community growth.

Positioning:

The aim is to have ARMN live, tradable and visible before Artemis mission coverage intensifies. Uniswap liquidity gives the token an open market venue while the project continues building toward wider exchange visibility.

### Phase 3: Smaller Exchange Listings and Community Growth

Objective: increase accessibility and build a durable holder community before larger exchange ambitions.

Key goals:

- Explore smaller centralized exchange listings.
- Expand Telegram, X/Twitter and short-form video presence.
- Publish regular mission updates and project updates.
- Build community campaigns around Artemis milestones.
- Add more educational content and simple tools.
- Improve token discoverability across wallets and token trackers.

Positioning:

This phase is about reach and credibility. Smaller exchange access, regular content and visible community activity help Artemis Moon move from a presale project to a recognizable lunar-era crypto brand.

### Phase 4: Utility, Auctions and Space-Themed Demand

Objective: create reasons for ARMN to be used, not only held.

Possible utility directions:

- Space-themed merchandise.
- Limited-edition digital collectibles.
- Auctions for physical memorabilia.
- Token-gated community access.
- Community competitions and mission prediction games.
- Partnerships with space educators, creators or merchandise suppliers.
- Long-term ambition around high-value prizes or experiences, such as space-related travel or events where legally and commercially practical.

Positioning:

The first utility layer should be achievable and visible. Auctions, merchandise and token-gated drops are more realistic early steps than promising direct access to the Moon. The project can still carry a bold long-term vision while executing simple utility first.

### Phase 5: Moon Economy and Space Marketplace

Objective: build Artemis Moon into a broader marketplace and cultural layer for future lunar and space missions.

Long-term possibilities:

- A marketplace for space and Moon-themed memorabilia.
- A platform for mission-linked collectibles and physical goods.
- Auctions tied to future space milestones.
- Creator and brand collaborations around lunar exploration.
- Community-funded space education or research initiatives.
- A broader "space economy" marketplace as commercial space activity grows.

Positioning:

The long-term vision is for ARMN to become a cultural and utility token for the space economy: a fixed-supply coin connected to Moon missions, memorabilia, collectibles, community events and future space-market activity.

## Messaging Principles

Use:

- "Aims to"
- "Targets"
- "Objective"
- "Planned"
- "Subject to market conditions"
- "Independent of NASA"
- "Verified contracts"
- "Fixed supply"
- "Built for the Artemis-era attention cycle"

Avoid:

- Guaranteed returns
- Guaranteed exchange listings
- NASA endorsement
- Claims that ARMN is an official Artemis token
- Claims that the token will increase in value
- Overpromising Moon trips or impossible prizes

## Summary

Artemis Moon should be presented as a project with phases:

1. Transparent presale.
2. Public launch and Uniswap liquidity.
3. Exchange accessibility and community growth.
4. Utility through auctions, merchandise and space-themed demand.
5. Long-term moon economy and space marketplace.

The key is to build credibility before hype, utility before overpromising, and traffic before the Artemis mission window becomes crowded.
